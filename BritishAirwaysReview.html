Business Task and Overview:

The goal was to create an interactive dashboard analyzing British Airways' customer review data to assess performance across key service metrics. This tool allows stakeholders to understand customer feedback and make data-driven decisions to enhance service quality and customer satisfaction.

Insights:

  1. Enhance In-Flight Entertainment:
    2. With the lowest average rating (1.4/5), entertainment needs significant improvements. Upgrading or diversifying entertainment options would likely boost passenger satisfaction across all demographics, particularly for business travelers (1.1/5 rating) who reported the lowest score.
  2. Revamp Food Service:
    3. Food received a low average rating of 2.4/5, with business travelers rating it the lowest at 1.9/5. Enhancing the quality and variety of meals and considering passenger feedback on meal preferences can improve this rating, especially for high-value business travelers.
  3. Address Regional Disparities:
    4. The wide range variation in country performance suggests targeted improvements are needed. For instance, countries with low average overall scores, such as Ghana (2.8/10) and India (3/10), may benefit from enhanced staff training, better ground services, or region-specific service improvements to align with expectations.
  4. Refine Services for Business Travelers:
    5. Business passengers reported below-average scores in key areas like cabin staff service (2.8/5) and ground service (2.6/5). Providing dedicated business services, more attentive cabin staff interactions, and steamlined ground operations could make a noteable impact on this valuable segment.
  5. Maintain and Replicate Best Practices:
    6. Analyzing what contributes to the high scored in top-performing countries like Czechia (9.5/10) and Turkey (8/10) can provide insights. Replicating successful strategies from these regions in underperforming areas could standardize higher-quality service across locations.
